    • Testing and Restoring backups (TempNand & NO$GBA)

    If you need help, ask the Nintendo DSiBrew Discord!
    I worked hard on this guide! If you want, you can donate through PayPal or Bitcoin!

    We will now install any modifications you've made on TempNand to your DSi.

    You MUST have the Flipnote Studio app on your DSi in order for this to work. Your console must also be a USA region console, due to the limitations of the Flipnote exploit. If your console is a USA region console, its system version should end with the letter "U".

    What you need

    • An SDHC (4-32 gigabytes) SD card for your DSi
    • The latest release of ugopwn, a DSiWare exploit for Flipnote Studio
    • The latest release of fwTool
    • The latest release of TempNand
    • The latest release of No$GBA
    • The Nintendo DSi BIOS

    Instructions

      Section I - Testing Your NAND with NO$GBA
    1. Download and extract NO$GBA to a directory.
    2. Extract the DSi BIOS files into the NO$GBA directory
    3. Do any modifications you want to your Nand with TempNand
    4. On TempNand, click File and then "Save for No$GBA". Navigate to the No$GBA folder and save it there with the name "DSI-1.mmc"
    5. Close the TempNand window and open No$GBA.
    6. Go to options and then Emulation setup.
    7. In the Emulation tab, set “Reset/Startup Entrypoint” to “GBA/NDS BIOS (Nintendo logo)” and NDS Mode Colors to “DSi (retail/16MB)”. Then click Save Now and then OK.
    8. Go to File, Cartridge Menu (FileName), and then open any .nds file.
    9. Your NAND will now be emulated by NO$GBA. Check that your NAND works perfectly
    10. If your encrypted NAND works on NO$GBA, then continue. If not, restart and find what went wrong!
    11. Insert your SD card into your computer if it's not in there already, then go back to TempNand and go to File > Save as
    12. Go back to the folder where you got your original nand backup from, and save the new one as nand_dsi.bin, overwriting the old one (You should still have a copy of the original that you saved in the earlier sections).
      13. Section II - Installing Your NAND
    13. Power off your DSi
    14. Insert your SD card into your computer
    15. Copy the contents of FWTool.zip to the root of your SD card and rename FWTool.nds to boot.nds
    16. Copy the contents of the ugopwn zip file to the root of your SD card.
    17. Remove the SD card from your computer and insert it into the SD card slot on your DSi
    18. Power on your device
    19. Go to System Settings > Data Management > Flipnote Studio (if you copy another DSiWare title, note that the .bin name will differ) > Copy > Yes and then exit to the DSi Menu
    20. Find the application named "Flipnote Studio" and open it.
    21. Choose "view flipnote", then choose "SD card"
    22. Choose "select folder", then choose "user" instead of "normal"
    23. Open the ugopwn folder
    24. Click on the note with the red bottom half, then choose "edit"
    25. Once you are on the editing page, click on the flipnote frog icon in the bottom left
    26. Click on the film roll icon, then choose "copy"
    27. Choose "back", then "exit"
    28. Click on the second note, then choose "edit"
    29. Click on the flipnote frog icon in the bottom left
    30. Click on the film roll icon
    31. Click on the single right arrow (the next to last arrow icon) two times. You should see a new frame be created.
    32. Now comes the hard part: Click on the paste button exactly 122 times
    33. Click the erase button, then the paste button
    34. Wait for FWTool to load. If it does not, you may have not renamed FWTool to boot.nds, or you may have clicked "paste" an incorrect number of times. If errors keep persisting, make sure your SD card is SDHC
    35. Once in FWTool, select Restore nand_dsi.bin (This may take a while. DO NOT EXIT FWTool until the restoration is complete.)
    36. Exit FWTool. You should now have your nand successfully installed on your DSi.
