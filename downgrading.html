    • Downgrading (ugopwn)

    We will now start downgrading to 1.4 system firmware.

    You MUST have the Flipnote Studio app on your DSi in order for this to work. Your console must also be a USA region console, due to the limitations of the Flipnote exploit. If your console is a USA region console, its system version should end with the letter "U".

    THERE IS A HIGH CHANCE OF BRICK WITH THE CURRENT METHOD, UNLESS YOU FOLLOW THESE INSTRUCTIONS CAREFULLY. ONLY YOU ARE RESPONSIBLE IF YOUR CONSOLE BRICKS. PROCEED WITH CAUTION. MOST IMPORTANTLY, DO NOT SKIP THE SECTION THAT DEALS WITH TESTING YOUR BACKUP, BECAUSE TESTING THE BACKUP IS THE EASIEST WAY TO PREVENT BRICKS.

    You MUST Download a torrent client to download some of the below files. One such as Deluge would work fine.

    What you need

    • An SDHC (4-32 gigabytes) SD card for your DSi
    • The latest release of ugopwn, a DSiWare exploit for Flipnote Studio
    • The latest release of fwTool
    • The latest release of HxD
    • The latest release of DSi SRL Extractor
    • The latest release of No$GBA
    • Codes.txt
    • The latest release of TWLTool
    • The latest release of NUSDownloader (with dsikey.bin generator)
    • The latest release of OSFMount
    • dsikey.bin (download torrent client to use magnet link)
    • The DSi footer template

    Instructions

    Section I - Dumping the Necessary Files
    1. Power off your device
    2. Insert your SD card into your computer
    3. Copy the contents of FWTool.zip to the root of your SD card and rename FWTool.nds to boot.nds
    4. Copy the contents of the ugopwn zip file to the root of your SD card.
    5. Remove the SD card from your computer and insert it into the SD card slot on your DSi
    6. Power on your device
    7. Go to System Settings > Data Management > Flipnote Studio (if you copy another DSiWare title, note that the .bin name will differ) > Copy > Yes and then exit to the DSi Menu
    8. Find the application named "Flipnote Studio" and open it.
    9. Choose "view flipnote", then choose "SD card"
    10. Choose "select folder", then choose "user" instead of "normal"
    11. Open the ugopwn folder
    12. Click on the note with the red bottom half, then choose "edit"
    13. Once you are on the editing page, click on the flipnote frog icon in the bottom left
    14. Click on the film roll icon, then choose "copy"
    15. Choose "back", then "exit"
    16. Click on the second note, then choose "edit"
    17. Click on the flipnote frog icon in the bottom left
    18. Click on the film roll icon
    19. Click on the single right arrow (the next to last arrow icon) two times. You should see a new frame be created.
    20. Now comes the hard part: Click on the paste button exactly 122 times
    21. Click the erase button, then the paste button
    22. Wait for FWTool to load. If it does not, you may have not renamed FWTool to boot.nds, or you may have clicked "paste" an incorrect number of times. If errors keep persisting, make sure your SD card is SDHC
    23. Select the options Dump CID and Dump nand_dsi.bin. This may take a while
    24. When both dumps are done, power off your device
      25. Section II - Downgrading your NAND
    25. Plug your SD card from your DSi into your PC
    26. Open your SD Card's drive and navigate to Private > DS > Title > 4B475545.bin and drag it to the same directory as dsi_srl_extract(make sure that you unzipped dsi_srl_extract.zip)
    27. Drag 4B475545.bin onto extract srl.bat
    28. Wait, and, when prompted, press any key to continue.
    29. You should see some new files
    30. Open HxD.exe and select the "Open" option
    31. Find the file named OUT.footer (in your dsi_srl_extract folder) and select "Open"
    32. You should see two blue lines of numbers and letters, one going up and down, and one going left to right
    33. Find the number "00000380" on the up-and-down line, and find "0F" on the left-to-right line. When you look at where they both lead, you should see two characters. Highlight them.
    34. Look to the right, and you should see a number that is also highlighted, followed by 15 other numbers below. Save all 16 of those (Not the pairs of letters and numbers!) to a text file to use for later. (This is called your Console ID)
    35. Open CID.bin in HxD (CID.bin is located in a folder of your SD Card with random letters and numbers!)
    36. Copy all the pairs of letters and numbers and paste it into a text file. (This is your CID, different from Console ID.)
    37. Open Codes.txt and replace "CID Here" and "ConsoleID Here" with your CID and ConsoleID (Make sure to delete the spaces of the CID! DO NOT DELETE THE [BRACKETS].)
    38. Save Codes.txt
    39. Open the directory of your SD Card, the folder with random letters and numbers, and copy nand_dsi.bin to the folder named twltool-v1.6 (make sure that you unzipped twltool-v1.6.zip)
    40. In the folder named twltool-v1.6, press shift and right click in an empty space and select "Open command window here" or "Open Powershell window here." Then paste in Code 1 (from our new Codes.txt) and press Enter
    41. When done, you should now have a file named NAND_DEC.bin
    42. With the DSi key torrent downloaded, place the file named dsikey.bin into the NUSDownloader folder when you finish generating the key.
    43. Open NUS Downloader.exe and check the box that says "Create Decryped Contents (*.app)"
    44. Select: Database > System (DSi) > System Menu (Launcher) > [Your Region] > v512
    45. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    46. When done, select: Database > System (DSi) > Nintendo DS Cart Whitelist > All > v256
    47. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    48. When done, select: Database > System (DSi) > System Settings > [Your Region] > v512
    49. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    50. When done, select: Database > System (DSi) > Version Data > [Your Region] > v4 (China)
    51. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    52. Install and open OSFMount
    53. Click the "Mount new..." button, followed by the "Image File" tick mark
    54. Press the button with the three periods (...) and select NAND_DEC.bin
    55. Select "Partition 0" when prompted. This file should be around 200 MB
    56. Uncheck the box that says "Read-Only Drive", and press OK
    57. Go to the "My Computer" directory, and you should see a new drive. Double-click it
    58. Delete anything inside of the "title\0003000f\484e4841\content" directory
    59. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "0003000f484e4841", and open the folder named "256"
    60. Copy "00000001.app" and "tmd.256" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\0003000f\484e4841\content" folder on your new drive in your computer
    61. Rename "tmd.256" to "title.tmd."
    62. Delete anything inside of the "title\00030017\484e4145\content" directory of the new drive.
    63. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "00030017484e41[XX]", and open the folder named "512"
    64. Copy "00000002.app" and "tmd.512" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\00030017\484e41[XX]\content" folder on your new drive in your computer
    65. Rename "tmd.512 to "title.tmd".
    66. Delete anything inside of the "title\00030015\484e4245\content" directory of the new drive.
    67. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "00030015484e42[XX]", and open the folder named "512"
    68. Copy "00000002.app" and "tmd.512" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\00030015\484e42[XX]\content" folder on your new drive in your computer
    69. Rename "tmd.512 to "title.tmd".
    70. Delete anything inside of the "title\0003000f\484e4c45\content" directory of the new drive.
    71. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "0003000f484e4c[XX]", and open the folder named "4"
    72. Copy "00000004.app" and "tmd.4" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\0003000f\484e4c[XX]\content" folder on your new drive in your computer
    73. Rename "tmd.4" to "title.tmd".
    74. Go to your OSFMount window, and press "Dismount". Exit OSFMount.
    75. In the folder named twltool-v1.6, press shift and right click in an empty space and select "Open command window here" or "Open Powershell Window here", then paste in Code 2 (from our new Codes.txt) and press Enter
    76. When it is done, you should see a file named "NAND_ENC.bin" in the "twltool-v1.6" folder
      77. Section III - Testing Your Downgraded NAND with NO$GBA
    77. Download and extract NO$GBA to a directory.
    78. Copy the NAND_ENC.bin file to your NO$GBA directory (make sure you have extracted the NO$GBA archive) and rename it to DSI-1.mmc
    79. Extract the DSi BIOS files from the "DSi firmware files.zip" archive into the NO$GBA directory
    80. Download the DSi footer template file and extract it to the NO$GBA directory.
    81. Open it in HxD.
    82. Replace the 16 bytes filed with AAs with your CID
    83. Replace the 8 bytes filed with BBs with your Console ID, but reversed. This means that if your Console ID starts with the byte 26 and ends with 08, for example, it should now end with 26 and start with 08.
    84. After you have inserted your CID and Console ID, highlight and copy the entire footer file.
    85. Open DSi-1.mmc and scroll to the end of the file. At the end of the file, paste in the footer.
    86. Save and close DSi-1.mmc and open No$GBA.
    87. Go to options and then Emulation setup.
    88. In the Emulation tab, set “Reset/Startup Entrypoint” to “GBA/NDS BIOS (Nintendo logo)” and NDS Mode Colors to “DSi (retail/16MB)”. Then click Save Now and then OK.
    89. Go to File, Cartridge Menu (FileName), and then open any .nds file (such as FWTool).
    90. Your NAND will now be emulated by NO$GBA. Check that your NAND works perfectly
    91. If your encrypted NAND works on NO$GBA, then rename the "NAND_ENC.bin" file to nand_dsi.bin (if it asks you to overwrite, you may want to move the other nand_dsi.bin somewhere else and try renaming it again)
    92. Move the new nand_dsi.bin to the folder in your SD card with random letters (if it asks to overwrite, simply accept)
      93. Section IV - Installing Your Downgraded NAND
    93. Follow steps 8-22 on section I of this page to re-open FWTool using ugopwn
    94. Once in FWTool again, select Restore nand_dsi.bin (This may take a while. DO NOT EXIT FWTool until the restoration is complete.)
    95. Exit FWTool. You should now be completely downgraded to version 1.4! You are ready to install HiyaCFW.
